Cinereria maritima

  • Scientific Name - Cinereria maritima
  • Common Name - Kallithi/कलिथि
  • Family- Asteraceae
  • Location - G20 lawn

Other Information:

commonly known as Dusty Miller, is a silver-gray foliaged perennial plant that is native to the Mediterranean region. Here are some important distinctive characteristics of this plant:

  1. Flower colour: The flowers of Cineraria maritima are small, daisy-like and yellow in color. They are borne in clusters on tall, wiry stems above the foliage.
  2. Flowering stage: Cineraria maritima typically blooms in mid- to late summer, producing flowers for several weeks. However, it is primarily grown for its foliage and not its flowers.
  3. Foliage: The leaves of Cineraria maritima are deeply lobed and covered in soft, silvery-gray hairs. They are typically used as a foliage plant in garden beds, borders, and containers, providing a striking contrast to other plants with green leaves.
  4. Size: Cineraria maritima can grow up to 1 to 2 feet tall and wide, making it a medium-sized plant.
  5. Growing conditions: Cineraria maritima prefers well-draining, sandy soil and full sun to partial shade. It is tolerant of drought and salt spray, which makes it suitable for coastal gardens.

Overall, Cineraria maritima is a visually striking plant with its silver-gray foliage, small yellow flowers, and ability to tolerate harsh growing conditions
