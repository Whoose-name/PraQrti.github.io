Ficus microcarpa

  • Scientific name - Ficus microcarpa
  • Common name - Weeping fig
  • Family - Moraceae
  • Location - Front lawn

Other Information:

Ficus microcarpa, also known as the Chinese Banyan, is a species of fig tree that is commonly used as an ornamental plant. Here are some important distinctive characteristics of this plant:

  1. Flower color: The flowers of Ficus microcarpa are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow in color.
  2. Flowering stage: The flowering stage of Ficus microcarpa usually occurs during the summer months, but can also occur at other times of the year.
  3. Bark: The bark of Ficus microcarpa is smooth and gray, with small lenticels (pores) scattered throughout.
  4. Leaves: The leaves of Ficus microcarpa are glossy, dark green, and oval-shaped, with a pointed tip. They are arranged alternately on the stem and can grow up to 10 cm in length.
  5. Fruits: Ficus microcarpa produces small, round, edible fruits that are green when young and turn yellowish-brown ripe.
  6. Aerial roots: Ficus microcarpa produces aerial roots that grow downwards from its branches, which can create a unique and interesting visual effect.
  7. Trunk shape: The trunk of Ficus microcarpa often has a distinctive, bulbous shape near its base, which can give the tree a striking appearance.
