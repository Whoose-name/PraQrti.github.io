Monoon longifolium

False ashoka

  • also commonly known by its synonym Polyalthia longifolia and Indian mast tree
  • It has a long history of cultural and religious significance and is known for its many uses in traditional medicine, cosmetics, and as a source of timber.
  • The Indian mast tree is a medium to large-sized tree that can grow up to 25 meters in height.
  • It has a straight, cylindrical trunk, and its bark is grayish-brown and smooth. The leaves are long and narrow, with a dark green color
  • tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.
  • Monoon longifolium is sometimes incorrectly identified as the ashoka tree (Saraca indica) because of the close resemblance of both trees. It can appear to have no branches

USES:-

  1. The tree also has many medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Indian medicine for centuries.
  2. The leaves and bark of the tree contain compounds that have anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, and anti-cancer properties.
  3. The tree is also used in cosmetics and personal care products, as its extracts are believed to have anti-aging and skin-soothing effects.
  4. In addition to its cultural and medicinal significance, the Indian mast tree is also valued for its timber.
  5. The wood of the tree is strong, durable, and resistant to decay, making it a popular choice for construction and furniture making.
  6. The tree is also used in reforestation projects and as a source of biofuels.

Cultural & Historical Importance:

  • The tree is considered sacred in Hinduism and is often planted near temples and other religious sites.
  • Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional religious ceremonies and festivals.
  • The tree is revered for its beauty, fragrance, and religious significance, and has been mentioned in Ramayana and the Mahabharata
  • In Hinduism, the Indian mast tree is considered sacred and is often planted near temples, shrines, and other religious sites.
  • It is believed to represent the goddess Saraswati, who is associated with knowledge, music, and the arts.
  • The leaves and flowers of the tree are used in traditional religious ceremonies and festivals, such as the Navaratri and Diwali
  • The tree is also associated with love and romance in Indian culture.
  • Its fragrant flowers are often used in bridal decorations, and it is believed that a garland made from the flowers of the Indian mast tree can bring good fortune to a newly married couple.

Plantation:-

  • planting

    Polyalthia longifolia prefers well-draining soils and partial sun exposure.

  • watering

    Regular watering is essential for Indian mast tree growth.

  • Temperature and humidity

    Indian mast tree grows in tropical to subtropical climates, prefers high humidity.

  • Fertilizer

    Polyalthia longifolia needs a balanced fertilizer, with equal amounts of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.

  • Soil

    The Indian mast tree prefers acidic to neutral soils with organic matter to improve drainage and fertility.

  • Pruning

    Polyalthia longifolia requires minimal pruning, but it is important to remove dead, diseased, or damaged branches regularly. Prune the tree during the dormant season to encourage new growth.
