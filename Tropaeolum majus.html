Tropaeolum majus

  • Scientific Name - Tropaeolum majus
  • Common Name - जल कुम्भी/nausturtium
  • Family- Tropaeolaceae
  • Location - G20 lawn

Other Information:

  1. Flower color: Nasturtium flowers are known for their bright and vibrant colors which range from yellow, orange, red, and sometimes even pink. The flowers typically have a contrasting dark center which adds to their beauty.
  2. Flowering stage: Nasturtiums are known for their extended flowering period which usually lasts from late spring to early fall. They have a long blooming period, often producing flowers continuously throughout the growing season.
  3. Distinctive foliage: Nasturtiums are also characterized by their unique foliage which is circular, flat and often lobed. The leaves can range in color from green to blue-green, and some varieties may have variegated or striped leaves.
  4. Edible flowers: One of the most distinctive characteristics of nasturtiums is that their flowers are edible. They have a slightly peppery taste and are often used as a garnish in salads and other dishes.
  5. Easy to grow: Nasturtiums are also known for being very easy to grow. They thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, and they don't require much water or fertilizer. They can also be grown in containers, making them a great choice for small gardens or balconies.
  6. Attracts pollinators: Nasturtiums are also great for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies to your garden, making them an excellent choice for those looking to support local ecosystems.
