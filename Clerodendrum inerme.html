Clerodendrum inerme

(anarmi)

  • Clerodendrum inerme, also known as Boll Plant, is a species of flowering shrub in the family Lamiaceae. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands

The plant contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties. The leaves and bark of the plant have been used to treat fever, coughs, colds, headaches, and other respiratory infections.

  3. Insect Repellent: Boll Plant has insect repellent properties and has been used traditionally to repel insects such as mosquitoes and flies. The crushed leaves or extracts from the plant have been used to create natural insect repellents.

  5. Biodiversity: Boll Plant is a host plant for the caterpillars of some butterfly species. Growing this plant can attract pollinators, such as butterflies and bees, to your garden and promote biodiversity
